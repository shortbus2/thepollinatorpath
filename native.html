Rabbit hole: plant labels

What does “native” mean?

When I first heard “native plant,” part of my brain translated it as: probably a weed. My brain was incorrect. It happens.

Home-field advantage

A native plant occurs naturally in a region and has a long ecological history there. It evolved alongside local insects, birds, soils, weather, and seasonal rhythms.

That history matters. Native plants can provide food, shelter, nesting material, and host relationships that some wildlife cannot easily replace with ornamental plants from elsewhere.

Native does not mean effortless

Colorado contains very different ecoregions. A plant native to a wet mountain meadow may still throw a full botanical tantrum in a hot, dry Front Range bed.

Right plant, right place still applies.
