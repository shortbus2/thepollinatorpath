Ecological change

A Yard Becoming Habitat

This is not a step-by-step renovation story. It is a simple record of what was here—and what is thriving now.

→
The answer

What changed was more than the view.

🐝 Native bees nested

Bare soil, stems, flowers, and undisturbed spaces became working habitat.

🌼 Food became continuous

Overlapping blooms now offer nectar and pollen across the growing season.

🐦 Birds found resources

Flowers, seed heads, water, shelter, and insects support visitors throughout the year.

🐸 Toads found shelter

Cool hiding places, water, leaf litter, and insect life made room for the night shift.

Wildlife is the proof

The garden stopped feeling like a collection of plants and started functioning as an ecosystem.
